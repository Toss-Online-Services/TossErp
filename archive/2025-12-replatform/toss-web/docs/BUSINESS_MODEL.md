# TOSS Business Model for South African SMMEs

## Mission

TOSS's mission is to **empower small businesses in townships and rural areas** with a modern platform that helps them run efficiently, grow sustainably, and connect to a broader business ecosystem.

In practical terms, TOSS provides an easy-to-use **ERP-III platform** (a next-generation business management system) enhanced by an **AI Copilot** ("Service-as-Software") and a **collaborative network** connecting shop owners with suppliers, drivers, and other partners.

### Our Goal

Level the playing field so that a spaza shop or chisa nyama in a village can operate with the same advantages as large formal retailers, while addressing the unique challenges of informal enterprises.

---

## The Problem

Township and rural SMMEs (like spaza shops and chisa nyamas) face severe challenges that keep them in "survival mode."

### Key Challenges

#### 1. Poor Cash Flow & No Financial Records
- Many operate on cash-only sales, limiting growth and creating risk
- Cash trading means no clear transaction records
- Hard to manage cash flow or prove creditworthiness
- Without proper bookkeeping, owners can't analyze finances or get loans
- Limited cash flow makes it hard to maintain balanced stock levels

#### 2. No Formal Stock Records
- Inventory management is often ad-hoc
- Most owners don't use formal stock-keeping tools
- Stock buying is reactive ("as and when needed") rather than planned
- Frequent stockouts due to poor visibility

#### 3. Limited Access to Credit
- Banks and lenders rarely extend credit to micro-businesses
- Lack of financial histories or collateral
- No credit score or formal footprint due to cash trading
- Trapped in a cycle of low investment and growth

#### 4. High Supply Costs
- Small shops lack bulk buying power
- Pay higher prices for stock (can't access volume discounts)
- Buy from cash-and-carry wholesalers in small batches
- Operating in isolation means no negotiating power
- Goods end up more expensive, squeezing margins

#### 5. No Delivery & Logistical Barriers
- Formal suppliers often don't deliver to township areas
- Hard-to-reach locations: no formal addresses, informal roads
- Security concerns in high-crime areas deter suppliers
- Shop owners must close to travel for restocking, losing sales time
- Inefficient last-mile supply chain leads to stockouts and higher prices

#### 6. Skills Gaps
- Many entrepreneurs lack formal business training
- Limited skills in bookkeeping, inventory management, marketing
- Low technology adoption
- Few use POS systems or accounting software
- Learning on the job, but mistakes can be costly

#### 7. "One-Man Show" Trust Issues
- Owners work alone or only with close family
- Fear of theft or cash leakage prevents hiring
- Operating informally makes it hard to form partnerships
- Limits growth and causes burnout
- Can't delegate tasks without supervision tools

#### 8. Low Trust and Financial Inclusion
- Broad trust deficit with formal institutions
- Many prefer cash and distrust banks or digital finance
- Don't leverage services like insurance, banking, or fintech
- Kept financially excluded from growth opportunities

### The Impact

> "Cash-based trading keeps spaza shops in survival modeâ€¦ without transparent systems to improve management, these businesses are shut out from growth opportunities."

These challenges result in stagnant businesses that just scrape by. Township SMMEs have the drive and market demand, but lack the tools, networks, and support to thrive.

## The Solution: ERP-III + AI Copilot + Collaborative Network

TOSS's solution has three integrated pillars:

### 1. ERP-III Platform (Next-Gen Business Management)

A modern, cloud-based system accessible on any device (especially smartphones) covering all core business functions in one place.

#### Key Features

**Point of Sale and Inventory Management**
- App serves as cash register and stock tracker
- Every sale recorded (even offline), updating stock counts
- Automated stock alerts when items are low
- Prevents stockouts and improves planning

**Purchasing & Supplier Integration**
- Order stock directly from partnered suppliers
- Suppliers plug into platform with product lists and prices
- All paperwork (invoices, receipts) handled digitally
- Builds transaction history for creditworthiness

**Financial Management**
- Tracks sales, expenses, and daily cash-ups
- Generates simple financial statements automatically
- Dashboard shows daily profit, best-selling items, cash flow
- Creates financial profile for accessing credit

**Mobile and Offline First**
- Works on low-end Android phones
- Caches data offline, syncs when possible
- As easy as using WhatsApp
- Available in local languages
- Minimal text input, more icon-based

**Customization for Local Needs**
- Modular design â€“ turn on what you need
- Flexible for different business types
- SaaS model with seamless updates
- Adapts to local regulations (e.g., tax rules)

#### What Makes It ERP-III

- **Cloud-based**: No expensive hardware needed
- **Modular**: Enable only what you need
- **Connected**: Links to supplier systems, payments, delivery networks
- **Scalable**: Updates roll out seamlessly
- **Collaborative**: Extends beyond the business to the ecosystem

### 2. "Service-as-Software" AI Copilot

The AI Copilot is embedded in the platform â€“ think of it as a 24/7 business assistant.

#### How It Works

**Intelligent Automation**
- Handles routine tasks automatically
- Analyzes sales trends and creates restock orders
- Notices patterns (e.g., cold drinks spike on Fridays)
- Like having a smart clerk monitoring constantly

**Decision Support**
- Trained on business best practices
- Provides recommendations in simple language
- Examples: "Your cash is low; deposit some for safety"
- "Bread nearing expiry, run a discount today"
- Bridges skills gap with expert guidance

**Natural Language Interaction**
- Chat with AI in English or vernacular
- Ask: "How do I handle credit sales?"
- Get helpful answers from TOSS knowledge base
- Lowers intimidation factor of software

**Autonomous Service Delivery**
- AI takes care of service outcomes, not just advice
- Acts as "AI bookkeeper" preparing tax filings
- Automates loan applications using data
- Like hiring extra hands that never sleep

**Continuous Learning**
- Gets smarter with more data
- Recommendations become more tailored
- Network-level learning from many users
- Shares insights across the platform

#### The Transformation

Even first-time entrepreneurs can operate like experienced pros. The Copilot:
- Turns data into action
- Helps make better decisions
- Automates dull tasks
- Provides a digital business partner

> "Traditional software makes the user do the work, but an AI agent can take responsibility for the outcome, like an accountant doing your taxes for you."

### 3. Collaborative Network Platform

TOSS isn't just an isolated app â€“ it's a collaborative platform linking many players in the SMME ecosystem.

#### Network Features

**Group Purchasing Power**
- Aggregates orders from many small businesses
- Example: 50 spaza shops need cooking oil this week
- Platform pools orders and negotiates bulk deal
- Even tiny shops enjoy volume discounts
- Directly reduces cost of goods
- Suppliers get large, organized orders with new customer reach

**Integrated Supply Chain & Delivery**
- Coordinates last-mile delivery to shops
- Local drivers get delivery requests via platform
- Suppliers drop bulk stock at TOSS depot
- Drivers handle "last mile" to each spaza
- Multiple shops' orders routed together efficiently
- Saves shop owners time and ensures stocked shelves

**Financial Services and Credit Access**
- Platform data (with consent) shared with financial partners
- Steady revenue records enable small working capital loans
- Suppliers can sell on credit with less risk
- Integration with DebiCheck for automated repayments
- Builds trust among network participants
- Brings informal traders into formal financial fold

**Collaboration & Community**
- Forums for entrepreneurs to ask questions and share tips
- Peer mentorship opportunities
- Connections to NGOs or government programs
- Training and business development resources
- Open ecosystem for new partners to plug in
- Platform as network orchestrator

#### Ecosystem Benefits

- **Shop owners**: Better prices, reliable service, access to credit
- **Suppliers**: Access to R178bn informal market via efficient channel
- **Drivers**: Earn income delivering goods
- **Communities**: Better-stocked shops with lower prices
- **Network effect**: Value increases as more participants join

## Business Model

### Revenue Streams

#### 1. Subscription Fees (Freemium Model)
- **Free Tier**: Basic POS and inventory for one shop
- **Premium Tier**: R200-500/month for multi-outlet, advanced analytics, extra AI features
- **Strategy**: Encourage adoption with free tier, monetize as businesses grow
- **Scalability**: Steady recurring revenue stream

#### 2. Transaction Commissions
- 3-5% commission on stock purchases through platform (from supplier side)
- Small fee on platform's logistics coordination
- **Value**: Suppliers willing to pay for sales they wouldn't get otherwise
- **Scalability**: Grows with transaction volume

#### 3. Financial Services & Partnerships
- Referral fees from connecting businesses to credit providers
- Interest spread on loans underwritten using TOSS data
- Inventory financing (TOSS fronts stock cost, shop repays with interest)
- **Value**: Monetizing unique financial data and credit worthiness profile

#### 4. Data Insights (Aggregated Analytics)
- Anonymized, aggregated market data valuable to FMCG companies, NGOs, policymakers
- Reports or API access for partners
- **Example**: Consumer goods company pays for insights on informal outlet sales
- **Scalability**: Secondary stream leveraging unique dataset

#### 5. Cross-Selling Add-ons
- Payroll module for shops hiring employees
- Micro-eCommerce module for online selling
- **Strategy**: Charge separately or bundle into higher tiers
- **Value Alignment**: Cost far lower than benefits/ROI for users

### Cost Structure

**Development**: Tech team, AI training costs

**Support**: Customer support/training via local agents or partnerships

**Subsidization**: Initial covering of delivery costs or free trials

**Advantage**: Software-centric model has inherent scalability â€“ serving 10,000 shops vs 1,000 doesn't linearly increase costs

### Key Resources

- **Technology**: Software platform and AI models
- **Network**: Partners (suppliers, logistics, financial services)
- **Data**: Transaction and operational data from network
- **Local Knowledge**: Understanding of township business realities
